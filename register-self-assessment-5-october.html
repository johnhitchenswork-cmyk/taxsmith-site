Register for Self Assessment by the 5 October deadline

If this is your first year working for yourself, or the first year your side income has grown past the point where it needs reporting, there is one date to get in your calendar: 5 October. That is the deadline to tell HMRC you need to file a Self Assessment tax return. Miss it and you can be left short of time to get set up properly. This guide explains who must register, exactly what the deadline means, how to register step by step, and what happens if you are late.

A plain UK guide for new sole traders, freelancers and side hustlers. General guidance, not formal tax advice.

Who must register

You generally need to register for Self Assessment if, in the tax year just gone, you fell into one of these groups:

  • New sole traders and freelancers. If you started working for yourself and are earning from it, you need to be in the Self Assessment system so HMRC can collect the Income Tax and Class 4 National Insurance on your profit.
  • Side hustlers over the trading allowance. If your gross side income for the prior tax year went over the 1,000 pound trading allowance and you are not already filing a return, you generally need to register.

Reporting rules for smaller side incomes are changing, so if you are close to the trading allowance it is worth confirming the current requirement on gov.uk before deciding you do or do not need to register.

What the 5 October deadline actually means

The 5 October deadline is about telling HMRC, not about filing or paying. The UK tax year runs to 5 April. If you first needed to register during a given tax year, the deadline to notify HMRC is the 5 October that follows the end of that tax year. So the trigger is which tax year you first became liable, and the deadline is the next 5 October after that year ends.

The mapping to remember: became liable during a tax year, tell HMRC by the 5 October after that tax year ends, then file and pay by the later Self Assessment deadlines.

How to register, step by step

  1. Go to gov.uk and register for Self Assessment. There is an online route for registering as self-employed or for other reasons. Choose the one that fits how you earn.
  2. Get your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR). HMRC sends this ten-digit reference by post after you register. You need it to file, so register in good time rather than at the last moment, because it is posted, not instant.
  3. Activate your online account. HMRC also posts an activation code for your online Self Assessment account. Enter it to finish setting up access.
  4. Keep your records from day one. From the moment you register, keep a clean record of income and allowable expenses, so that when the return is due the figures are ready and accurate.

Because the UTR and activation code arrive by post, the practical advice is to register as early as you can once you know you need to, not to wait until the 5 October deadline is nearly on you.

What happens if you are late

Registering after 5 October does not automatically trigger a penalty on its own, but it puts you under time pressure. You still need your UTR to arrive, you still need to file, and you still need to pay by the Self Assessment deadlines that follow. Penalties can apply if you miss those later filing or payment dates. Leaving registration late is how people end up filing in a rush or missing a deadline they did not have to. The clean approach is to register as soon as you know you are liable and confirm any penalty position on gov.uk.

FAQ

What is the 5 October deadline?

It is the deadline to tell HMRC you need to complete a Self Assessment tax return, if you have newly become self-employed or your side income has gone over the trading allowance. It falls on the 5 October following the end of the tax year in which you first needed to register.

How do I register as self-employed with HMRC?

Register online on gov.uk under Self Assessment. HMRC sets you up for a return and posts a Unique Taxpayer Reference, then an activation code for your online account. Allow time for these to arrive.

What if I register late?

Registering after 5 October is not an automatic penalty, but it leaves less time to get your UTR and file, and penalties can apply if you then miss the filing or payment deadlines. Register as soon as you know you need to.

Do side hustlers need to register?

Generally yes, if gross side income went over the 1,000 pound trading allowance in the prior tax year and you are not already filing. Confirm the current position on gov.uk, as reporting rules are changing.
