How the calculator works

The maths follows the same steps HMRC uses, in the same order. First it works out your profit by subtracting your allowable expenses from your income. Then it applies Income Tax and Class 4 National Insurance to that profit. What is left is your take-home pay.

Income Tax

Everyone starts with a personal allowance of 12,570 pounds, on which no Income Tax is due. Above that, profit is taxed at 20 percent up to 50,270 pounds, 40 percent from there up to 125,140 pounds, and 45 percent above 125,140 pounds. Once profit passes 100,000 pounds the personal allowance is reduced by 1 pound for every 2 pounds over that figure, which is why very high earners lose it entirely by 125,140 pounds. The calculator handles this taper automatically.

Class 4 National Insurance

Class 4 is the National Insurance the self-employed pay on profit. It is charged at 6 percent on profit between 12,570 pounds and 50,270 pounds, then 2 percent on anything above 50,270 pounds. It sits on top of Income Tax and is a common thing to forget when setting money aside.

Class 2 National Insurance: why it is zero

This is the point many tools get wrong. Since 6 April 2024, self-employed people with profits at or above the Small Profits Threshold, 6,845 pounds in 2025/26 and 7,105 pounds in 2026/27, have Class 2 treated as paid at no cost. There is no mandatory Class 2 charge for a profitable sole trader. Below that threshold, Class 2 is voluntary if you want to keep your National Insurance record complete, at 3.50 pounds a week in 2025/26 and 3.65 pounds a week in 2026/27. This calculator charges zero for mandatory Class 2, which is the correct figure.

FAQ

How much tax does a sole trader pay?

A sole trader pays Income Tax and Class 4 National Insurance on profit, which is income minus allowable expenses. Income Tax is 20 percent above the 12,570 pound personal allowance, 40 percent above 50,270 pounds and 45 percent above 125,140 pounds. Class 4 is 6 percent between 12,570 and 50,270 pounds, then 2 percent above. There is no mandatory Class 2 charge.

Do I pay National Insurance if I am self-employed?

Yes, Class 4 National Insurance, at 6 percent on profit between 12,570 and 50,270 pounds and 2 percent above. Class 2 is treated as paid for the self-employed above the Small Profits Threshold, so there is nothing mandatory to pay for it.

Is Class 2 National Insurance still charged?

Not as a mandatory charge. Since 6 April 2024 it is treated as paid at no cost for the self-employed above the Small Profits Threshold, and is voluntary below it. This calculator correctly shows zero.

How is take-home pay worked out for the self-employed?

Take income, subtract allowable expenses to get profit, then subtract Income Tax and Class 4 National Insurance. What is left is your take-home pay, shown here per year, month and week.
