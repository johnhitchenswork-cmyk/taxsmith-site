Do you owe tax on your side hustle?

A side hustle should not come with a nagging worry about HMRC. This short checker walks you through the two questions that decide whether your side income needs reporting, then gives you a plain answer and what to do next. It is built around the current 1,000 pound trading allowance, and it flags where the rules are changing so you know to confirm the latest position before you act.

A quick UK guide for people with a second income alongside a job or their main work. General guidance, not formal tax advice.

The two-question checker

Question 1. Is your gross side income this tax year (before any expenses) under or over the 1,000 pound trading allowance?

Question 2. Do you already file a Self Assessment tax return for another reason (for example you are already registered as self-employed, or a higher earner who files)?

What the trading allowance is

The trading allowance lets you earn up to 1,000 pounds of gross self-employed or casual income in a tax year without paying tax on it, and usually without needing to report it. The word gross matters: it is 1,000 pounds of income before you take off any expenses, not 1,000 pounds of profit. If everything you made from the side hustle across the whole tax year is under that figure, you are generally in the clear.

When you need to register

If your gross side income for a tax year goes over the 1,000 pound trading allowance, and you are not already in Self Assessment for another reason, you generally need to register with HMRC. The deadline to register is 5 October after the end of the tax year in which you crossed the threshold. Because the UK tax year ends on 5 April, crossing the line in one tax year means registering by the 5 October that follows.

A note on the changing rules

The reporting rules for side income are in the middle of a change. HMRC has signalled a higher threshold, below which a Self Assessment return may not be required, even though tax can still be owed on profit above the 1,000 pound trading allowance. Because this is moving, this page deliberately does not state a settled new figure. The safe approach is to confirm the current threshold and reporting requirement on gov.uk at the moment you need to decide, rather than relying on an article.

Selling online: trading or not

A common worry is selling on online marketplaces. The dividing line is whether you are trading. Selling your own unwanted possessions, clearing out a wardrobe or a garage, is usually not taxable. Buying or making things specifically to sell at a profit is trading, and if that gross income passes the trading allowance it may need reporting, with tax due on the profit above the allowance.

FAQ

What is the 1,000 pound trading allowance?

It lets you earn up to 1,000 pounds of gross side income in a tax year without tax on it and, usually, without reporting it. It is measured on income before expenses, not on profit.

When must I register for Self Assessment for a side hustle?

Generally when your gross side income passes the 1,000 pound trading allowance and you are not already filing. The registration deadline is 5 October after the end of that tax year. Confirm the current rules on gov.uk, as they are changing.

Do I pay tax on selling online?

Selling your own unwanted items is usually not taxable. Buying or making things to sell for profit is trading, and if gross income passes the trading allowance, tax may be due on the profit above it.

What is the deadline to register?

5 October following the end of the tax year in which your side income first went over the trading allowance.
