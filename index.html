Questions self employed people ask

What does TaxSmith actually do?

TaxSmith is a UK tax calculator for self employed people. You enter your income figures and it gives you an estimate of the income tax and National Insurance you are likely to owe, along with your estimated take home pay. It is built to give you a clear early picture so you can plan, rather than waiting for the self assessment bill to surprise you.

Who is it for?

It is built for sole traders, freelancers, contractors and side hustle owners in the UK. Anyone whose income is not taxed at source through a normal payslip can use it to picture their tax position.

Is this the same as filing my tax return?

No. TaxSmith gives you an estimate to help you plan and set money aside. Filing your self assessment return is done with HMRC. TaxSmith is the planning step that means you reach that point prepared, with a sense of what to expect.

Does it cover National Insurance as well as income tax?

Yes. Self employed National Insurance is one of the parts people most often overlook. TaxSmith includes it in your estimate so the figure you see is closer to the full picture, not just income tax on its own.

Where do I get it?

TaxSmith is on the App Store for iPhone. Tap the Download on the App Store badge on this page to go straight to the listing and install it.

Should I still check figures with HMRC or an accountant?

Yes. TaxSmith gives a helpful estimate for planning. For anything you are unsure about, or for your final return, check the current rules on the HMRC website or speak to a qualified accountant. Tax rules and thresholds can change, so treat the estimate as guidance rather than a definitive figure.
